Workflow snapshots

What people actually open it for.

The product is strongest in the small gaps between work, not during long setup sessions.

You finish a deep work block and need to re-enter the schedule without losing too much attention. The popup gives you immediate situational awareness.

  • Check the day strip and current timeline position.
  • See whether the next event is close or still far enough away.
  • Return to work or start preparing based on the chip and event placement.

Right before a meeting, the fastest path is not “open Google Calendar and start digging.” It is “open the popup and act from there.”

  • Confirm the event title, time, and status in one glance.
  • Use the event area as the transition into the correct meeting.
  • Keep the context-switch smaller, especially on stacked call days.

When the scheduling task is small, users should not pay the cost of a full calendar detour. The popup stays useful for fast planning decisions.

  • Open day, week, or month depending on the question.
  • Use the add action for small scheduling moves.
  • Open Google Calendar only when the planning task becomes more complex.
