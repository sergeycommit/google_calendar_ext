# The Google Calendar Settings Nobody Tells You to Change

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · October 23, 2026*

---

Most people set up Google Calendar once, accept whatever defaults came with it, and never go back to settings. I was one of those people for years.

Then I spent an hour in the settings panel — actually reading, not just glancing — and found a handful of options that changed how the calendar works in small but meaningful ways. None of them are obvious. All of them are worth knowing.

## The settings worth changing

**Speedy meetings.** Under Settings → General → Event settings. Turns 60-minute meetings into 50-minute meetings and 30-minute ones into 25-minute ones by default. The first day you use it you'll regain 30–40 minutes of buffer time without negotiating a single meeting.

**Default event duration.** The default is 60 minutes. Most of your meetings are probably not 60 minutes. Change it to 30 or 45 and see how much scheduling friction drops.

**Default event visibility.** If you're in a shared workspace, this determines whether your events are visible to colleagues by default. "Public" is the right choice if you want team coordination to be easy.

**World clock.** Under Settings → General → World clock. Add two or three frequently-used time zones. They appear as a sidebar in the weekly view, showing the current time in each zone. Essential for anyone who schedules across regions.

**Notification defaults.** The default 10-minute reminder can be changed to whatever makes sense for how you work — 30 minutes if you need prep time, 5 minutes if you just need a prompt to show up.

**Alternate calendars.** You can display lunar, Hebrew, Islamic, and other calendar systems alongside the standard Gregorian one — useful for international coordination.

## The working hours setting

Under Settings → Working hours. Defining your working hours does two things: it shows colleagues that you're not available outside those windows, and it can trigger a warning when someone tries to schedule you outside them.

For anyone with a non-standard schedule or who works across time zones with colleagues in different hours, this is significant.

> Most of these settings are fire-and-forget — you change them once and they work silently. The 20 minutes it takes to audit your Calendar settings is one of the better-ROI configuration investments you can make.

**This week's takeaway:**

- World clock sidebar is essential for anyone scheduling across time zones
- Working hours setting communicates availability and triggers warnings for out-of-hours scheduling
