# You Probably Don't Know Your Own Calendar as Well as You Think

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · October 16, 2026*

---

I needed to find an old meeting from six months ago — one where a decision was made that now, apparently, contradicted a current decision. I knew it had happened. I had no idea when, or what it was called, or who else was there.

Google Calendar's search found it in about 15 seconds.

This is one of those features people discover years into using a product and immediately wonder how they managed without it. The calendar isn't just a forward-looking scheduler. It's a record of where your time went — and search is how you access that record.

## The basics worth knowing

The search bar at the top of Google Calendar accepts event titles, descriptions, attendee names, and locations. Typing a person's name shows every meeting you've had with them. Typing a project keyword shows every event connected to that project.

This seems obvious, but most people never use it — they scroll instead, which is hopeless once you're looking for something more than a few weeks back.

## Search filters that change what you can find

**Date range:** You can filter results to a specific time window. "Last quarter" or a custom range.

**Calendar source:** If you have multiple calendars (work, personal, team), you can filter search to a specific one.

**People:** Search by attendee name to see your meeting history with a specific colleague, client, or partner.

**Has attachment:** Filter for events with attached documents — useful when you're looking for a meeting where a specific file was shared.

## Practical uses I've found

**Reconstructing decisions:** "When did we decide to use vendor X?" Search the vendor name. Find the meeting. The notes are in the event description (if you kept them there).

**Tracking patterns:** "How many times did I meet with this client in Q3?" Search their name, filter by Q3. Count the results.

**Finding recurring meetings you've forgotten about:** Sometimes a meeting was created months ago and has been quietly running. Searching the organizer's name reveals it.

> Your calendar is a more complete record of your professional life than most people realize. The work is already done — search just makes it accessible.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Calendar search covers titles, descriptions, attendee names, and locations — not just event titles
- Search by person's name to see your full meeting history with them
- Date range and calendar filters make the results immediately useful
