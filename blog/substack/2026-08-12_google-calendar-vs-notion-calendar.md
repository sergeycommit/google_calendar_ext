# Google Calendar vs. Notion Calendar: Do You Need to Switch?

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · December 17, 2027*

---

Notion Calendar (formerly Cron) is one of the most thoughtfully designed calendar applications I've used. Every interaction is deliberate. The keyboard shortcuts work. The visual hierarchy is clear. It's a genuinely better-looking product than Google Calendar.

It's also built on top of Google Calendar — which raises the question of whether you need it, or whether you're adding a layer of sophistication over infrastructure you're not fully using yet.

## What Notion Calendar adds over Google Calendar

**Better keyboard navigation.** Notion Calendar's shortcuts are more extensive and more consistent than Google Calendar's. For keyboard-first users, this is a real advantage.

**Cleaner visual design.** Less noise, better typography, more intentional use of space. If aesthetics matter to your daily interaction with the calendar, Notion Calendar is superior.

**Better event creation UX.** Natural language parsing and a cleaner creation flow make quick event creation faster.

**Meeting prep surface.** Notion Calendar can pull in relevant documents and meeting notes alongside your events, reducing the context-switching to find prep materials.

## What it doesn't change

**The underlying data.** Notion Calendar syncs with Google Calendar. Your events, attendees, and recurring meetings are all the same. You're changing the interface, not the infrastructure.

**Sharing and permissions.** Still managed in Google Calendar. Notion Calendar doesn't surface team calendar sharing.

**Integrations.** Third-party integrations (Zoom auto-add, etc.) are configured in Google Calendar and carry over.

## The honest recommendation

If you're already using Google Calendar well — time blocking, color coding, recurring event maintenance, calendar sharing — Notion Calendar's improvements are genuine but marginal. If you're not using Google Calendar well, a new interface won't fix that.

If you do a lot of keyboard-driven calendar work and value visual design, Notion Calendar is worth a trial. Otherwise, spend the adoption energy on habits.

> A better interface to the same data and the same habits produces marginal improvement. Better habits with either interface produce substantial improvement.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Notion Calendar is a better interface over Google Calendar data, not a replacement
- Real advantages: keyboard navigation, visual design, event creation UX, meeting prep
- Habit quality matters more than interface quality — fix the habits first
