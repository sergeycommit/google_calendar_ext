# Scheduling Across Time Zones Without Turning It Into a Full-Time Job

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · May 28, 2027*

---

There's a specific kind of email thread that makes me tired just looking at it: the "finding a time to meet" thread with five people across four time zones. Ten messages. Nobody agrees on the conversion. One person is on DST and one isn't. Someone proposes a time that's after 9pm for another person. Eventually a time gets picked that's mildly inconvenient for everyone.

This workflow is broken. It can be fixed.

## The tools that handle this

**Scheduling links (Calendly-type).** Share a link; the recipient picks from your available slots. They see your availability in their local timezone automatically. The time zone math happens inside the tool.

**Google Meet's timezone-aware invites.** When you create a Google Calendar event with international attendees, it displays in each attendee's local timezone. The invite shows "4:00 PM EDT / 9:00 PM BST / 5:00 AM JST (following day)" — all in one place.

**World clock sidebar during manual scheduling.** When manually choosing a time, the world clock sidebar in Google Calendar shows current local time for saved zones simultaneously. You can see at a glance whether 3pm your time works for everyone else.

## The practices that reduce friction

**Anchor on one timezone for the "official" time.** Pick the timezone where most people or the meeting organizer is located. Communicate the conversion, but clarify which one is canonical.

**Include timezone in the invite title for unusual schedules.** "Product sync (2pm PT / 5pm ET / 10pm CET)" in the event title is redundant for many but catches errors when someone's system doesn't auto-convert.

**Set up scheduling links before you need them.** Having a booking link ready reduces the response time from "let me find a time that works" to "here's my availability link."

> The cost of bad timezone coordination is measured in wasted time, missed starts, and the gradual resentment that comes from always being the person who joins at midnight.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Scheduling links with automatic timezone conversion eliminate the round-trip email thread
- Google Calendar shows times in each attendee's local zone — use it, don't add manual conversions
- Set up your scheduling tool before you need it; having it ready changes how quickly you can respond to scheduling requests
