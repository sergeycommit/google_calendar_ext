# Setting Up Out of Office the Right Way (Most People Don't)

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · November 13, 2026*

---

I used to handle vacation coverage the same way most people do: send an email to everyone who might need me, set up an out-of-office auto-reply, and hope nothing important fell through the gap.

The calendar-first approach is better. When your out-of-office is configured properly in Google Calendar, it handles much of the communication automatically — before people even send a message.

## What Google Calendar's Out of Office feature actually does

When you create an Out of Office event in Google Calendar (available under the + button, labeled "Out of office"), it does several things beyond just blocking time:

**Meeting decline automation:** Any meeting invitation that arrives while you're marked Out of Office gets automatically declined. The sender receives a notification explaining why, without you needing to do anything.

**Custom decline message:** You can set a message that goes with the automatic decline. "I'm out of office from Nov 14–21, returning Nov 22. For urgent items, contact [colleague]" is significantly more useful than a generic decline.

**Visibility to colleagues:** Your calendar shows the Out of Office block to anyone with access to your calendar, so scheduling conflicts are visible before people even send an invite.

## Setting it up properly

Create the Out of Office event spanning the full period. In the event details, write a meaningful decline message — include: the return date, who to contact for urgent matters, and any specific context about what will or won't be covered.

If you have a backup person during your absence, consider delegating limited calendar access to them so they can see what's coming in.

## The email layer

A calendar Out of Office doesn't replace your email auto-reply — you need both. But they should be consistent. Same return date, same contact for urgency, same tone. A confused auto-reply that contradicts your calendar status undermines both.

> The goal of an out-of-office setup is to give people enough information to make a decision without waiting for you. Good coverage information removes the uncertainty that drives follow-up pings.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Google Calendar's Out of Office feature auto-declines meetings and sends custom messages to organizers
- Include return date, urgent contact, and specific context in the decline message — not just "I'm away"
- OOO calendar event + email auto-reply should be consistent and mutually reinforcing
