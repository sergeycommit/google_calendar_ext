# Managing 4 Time Zones on One Calendar Without Losing My Mind

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · April 30, 2027*

---

I work with people in San Francisco, New York, London, and Tokyo. My calendar has events scheduled across all four time zones, and for the first year I managed this by doing the mental arithmetic every time I scheduled something.

That was unsustainable and error-prone. I showed up to meetings late (in my time zone) twice because I confused AM/PM on a 12-hour clock conversion.

## Google Calendar's built-in timezone tools

Google Calendar has a world clock sidebar that I didn't discover until embarrassingly late. Under Settings → General → World clock, you can add up to 4 time zones. They appear as a sidebar in the weekly view, showing the current local time for each zone.

When creating an event, you can specify the event time zone — useful when a colleague sends an invite in their local time and you want to create a corresponding event in yours.

## The world clock sidebar as the daily tool

The sidebar is the most useful thing for day-to-day scheduling. Instead of doing timezone math:

1. I know I'm scheduling something
2. I look at the sidebar
3. I can see what time it will be for each team simultaneously

This is faster, more reliable, and eliminates mental arithmetic errors.

## For teams with many timezones

If you frequently schedule across four or more zones, a few practices help:

**Use GMT offsets in event titles** when you think there might be ambiguity: "Sync (3pm PT / 6pm ET / 11pm London)." Five extra characters prevent one person showing up at the wrong time.

**Declare one reference timezone for the team.** Usually wherever most people or the HQ is. Everyone converts to that reference when discussing meeting times informally.

**Set your "secondary timezone" in Calendar settings.** You can display a second time column alongside your primary timezone in the weekly view — useful if you have one primary partner timezone alongside your local one.

> Time zone coordination is a tax you pay for global teams. Tools don't eliminate it; they reduce the mental effort of each individual transaction.
