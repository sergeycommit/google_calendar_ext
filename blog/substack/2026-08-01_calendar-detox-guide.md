# The Calendar Detox: Cleaning House When the Schedule Is Out of Control

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · October 1, 2027*

---

Every few months, the calendar gets away from me. Meetings accumulate. Recurring events that should have been cancelled are still running. The week looks full but nothing important is getting done. The schedule is busy but not effective.

When this happens, a calendar detox is the fix. Not a tweak — a reset.

## Signs a calendar detox is needed

- You open Monday morning and feel immediately overwhelmed
- You haven't had a focused work block in two weeks
- More than 30% of your meetings feel optional or unclear in purpose
- The calendar has events from projects that ended months ago
- You can't quickly articulate what your three most important commitments are this week

## The detox process

**Block two hours.** Put it on the calendar. Call it "Calendar reset." Treat it as a project.

**Export a list of all recurring events.** Open the calendar sidebar and list every recurring meeting. This is probably more than you think.

**Apply the 30-second filter.** For each recurring event: "Can I name what would break if this stopped, in 30 seconds?" Yes → keep. No → cancel or investigate.

**Cancel or reduce everything that failed the filter.** Reduction (weekly to bi-weekly, hour to 30 minutes) often feels more socially comfortable than cancellation and achieves most of the benefit.

**Clear the solo blocks.** Which focus blocks and work blocks are on the calendar that no longer correspond to active work? Delete them.

**Rebuild from the list.** What are your current priorities? Schedule explicit blocks for them. What are your non-negotiable recurring commitments? Confirm they're there. Don't add anything else until week one is done.

> The goal isn't an empty calendar — it's a calendar that reflects current priorities rather than accumulated history. After a detox, the calendar should be readable and feel manageable.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- The 30-second filter: if you can't name what breaks when a meeting stops, it's a candidate for cancellation
- Calendar detox = audit recurring events + cancel/reduce stale ones + rebuild from current priorities
- Do this quarterly or when the calendar stops feeling like a useful tool
