# The Calendar Setup That Actually Works for Small Teams

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · January 7, 2028*

---

Small team calendar coordination is a specific problem. Large enterprises have IT departments, calendar admins, and standardized tool stacks. Solo workers have full control. Small teams — 3 to 15 people — sit in an awkward middle where coordination matters but bureaucracy doesn't help.

## The small team calendar problems

**Scheduling across people is slow.** Without visibility into each other's calendars, finding time for three people requires an email thread or a scheduling poll that adds days to every decision.

**Meeting accumulation.** Small teams often have too many meetings relative to the team's capacity because the informal coordination happens in meetings rather than async.

**No centralized team calendar.** Important shared events — launches, planning sessions, offsites, holidays — live in someone's personal calendar and aren't reliably visible to the team.

## The setup that solves these problems

**Shared free/busy visibility.** Every team member shares their Google Calendar with the team at "see free/busy" level minimum. This is a two-minute setup and eliminates scheduling email threads.

**A team calendar layer.** Create a shared Google Calendar called "Team" that everyone subscribes to. Major shared events, milestones, PTO, holidays — all go here. It sits alongside individual calendars in everyone's view.

**A team scheduling link.** Tools like Calendly for Teams or Cal.com team features allow booking time with multiple team members at once. For cross-functional meetings with clients or partners, this is faster than the round-trip availability exchange.

**A weekly team planning event.** A short (30-min) weekly sync where the team looks at the coming week together. Not a status meeting — a planning session. Who's heads-down? Who's available for collaboration? Any shared conflicts?

> Small team coordination doesn't require sophisticated tools. It requires shared visibility, one shared calendar layer, and one weekly planning moment.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Shared free/busy visibility is the minimum viable setup for small team coordination
- A shared team calendar for milestones and PTO creates the "big picture" view everyone needs
- Weekly planning sync (30 min) aligns the team without requiring constant ad-hoc coordination
