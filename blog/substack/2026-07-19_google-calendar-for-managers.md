# How Managers Should Think About Their Calendar Differently

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · July 2, 2027*

---

An individual contributor's calendar and a manager's calendar serve different purposes. When you manage a team, the calendar is no longer just a schedule for your own work — it's a communication device, a signal about organizational priorities, and a direct input into other people's ability to do their jobs.

The habits that work for an IC can actively backfire for managers.

## The manager's calendar is a signal

When a manager has four focus blocks per day but none for 1-on-1s, the signal to the team is: individual work takes priority over people. When a manager's calendar is entirely packed with meetings, the signal is: there's no bandwidth for the unexpected. When a manager's calendar is consistently empty and available, the signal is: I'm always interruptable, which becomes an invitation to interrupt.

Managers who understand this calibrate their calendar not just for their own productivity but for the team's perception of what's important.

## The structures that work for managers

**Protected time for 1-on-1s.** Weekly or bi-weekly 1-on-1s that never get moved except for genuine emergencies. Their regularity is part of the value — people can count on them.

**Office hours.** A recurring open slot where team members can book time without a formal agenda. Signals availability without requiring the manager to be always-on.

**Decision-making blocks.** Managers make many decisions daily. A structured time for reviewing requests, making calls, and clearing blockers prevents these from getting lost in the margin between meetings.

**Deep work blocks — but fewer of them.** Most management work requires context-switching. That's the job. But a few protected hours per week for reading, writing, and thinking are still valuable — they're just a smaller proportion of the week than for an IC.

> The manager's calendar is semi-public. How you structure it communicates values to your team more clearly than most things you say explicitly.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- A manager's calendar is a signal to the team about priorities, not just a personal schedule
- Protected 1-on-1s and office hours communicate investment in the team
- Fewer deep work blocks than an IC, but some protected thinking time is still necessary
