# My Calendar Notification Setup (After 6 Years of Getting It Wrong)

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · September 25, 2026*

---

For most of my professional life, I used the default notification settings in Google Calendar. Events reminded me 10 minutes before — which sounds sensible. In practice, it meant I was interrupted 20–30 times a day by reminders for events that ranged from critical meetings to a dentist appointment I'd already thought about three times that week.

The notifications weren't serving me. They were taxing me.

## The problem with default notifications

The default is a compromise. Ten minutes might be right for a meeting that requires zero preparation. It's wrong for a meeting where you need to find a document, pull up context, and arrive mentally ready. It's also overkill for a calendar note you put there as a reference, not a reminder.

The other issue: desktop + mobile + email notifications for the same event is three interruptions for one calendar item. Most people have all three on and wonder why their phone feels like a news ticker.

## The setup I settled on

**Important meetings:** Two reminders — 30 minutes before (to prepare), 5 minutes before (to actually show up). The 30-minute one is the one that matters.

**Routine recurring meetings:** One reminder, 5 minutes before. These don't need prep; they just need attendance.

**Work blocks and personal events:** No notification. I check my calendar at the start of the day and at the start of each hour. I don't need a ping to tell me I'm supposed to be working on the thing I planned to work on.

**Deadlines:** A reminder the evening before and the morning of. Not a 10-minute warning — I can't do anything useful about a deadline at T-minus 10 minutes.

## The channel configuration

I turned off email notifications entirely. If something is on my calendar, I will see it when I check my calendar. Email notifications create a second stream of the same information I already have.

Mobile notifications: only for meetings that start in the next 10 minutes. Not every reminder — only the imminent ones.

Desktop notifications: off for everything except meetings I'm about to join. A 10-minute desktop popup in the middle of deep work is a context-switch tax I stopped paying.

> The test for any notification: does receiving this interrupt justify the interruption cost? Most default notifications fail this test.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Default notifications are optimized for general cases, not your specific ones — customize them
- 30-minute reminders are more valuable than 10-minute ones for meetings requiring preparation
- Turn off redundant channels: email + desktop + mobile for the same event is three interruptions for no extra benefit
