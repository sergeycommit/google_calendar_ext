# My Sunday 20-Minute Weekly Planning Ritual

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · November 27, 2026*

---

I spent three years starting every Monday by opening my calendar and reacting to what was there. Then I started spending 20 minutes on Sunday evening doing the opposite: looking at what was coming and making decisions about it before the week started.

The Monday-morning version of me is grateful for the Sunday-evening version of me every single week.

## Why the timing matters

Planning on Sunday has one advantage over planning on Monday morning: you're not yet in the week. You're not already behind on emails, already running late to something, already stressed about a meeting that starts in 45 minutes.

Sunday planning is calm, forward-looking, and unhurried. Monday planning is reactive, tactical, and incomplete because the day has already started.

## The 20-minute structure

**Minutes 1–5: Review the week ahead.**  
Open Google Calendar. Look at the full week. Note all meetings: when they are, what they require, who's there. Flag any that need preparation.

**Minutes 6–10: Check the task list.**  
What are the 3–5 most important things to accomplish this week? These are usually a mix of project deliverables, decisions, and maintenance items.

**Minutes 11–16: Block time for the important work.**  
Match the important tasks to open windows in the calendar. Create blocks with specific names. This is where the week's work gets protected — or doesn't.

**Minutes 17–20: Resolve any conflicts.**  
Any meetings that overlap with important blocks? Decide now whether to move the meeting, move the block, or accept the constraint. Deciding on Sunday costs less stress than deciding Monday morning.

## What I don't do in the review

I don't look at email. I don't check Slack. The review is about the calendar and the task list — two inputs, one output (a planned week). Any urgent messages can wait until Monday; most things that feel urgent on Sunday aren't.

> The goal isn't a perfect week. It's a week that has been thought about before it starts, so the first surprise on Monday isn't "I have four back-to-back meetings and nothing is prepared."

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Sunday planning is more effective than Monday planning because you're not yet in reactive mode
- Structure: review the week → check task list → block important work → resolve conflicts
- 20 minutes invested on Sunday pays in clarity every day of the following week
