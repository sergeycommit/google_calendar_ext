# Why I Switched From Heavy Calendar Apps to Lightweight Tools

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · April 16, 2027*

---

I spent two years chasing the perfect calendar setup. I tried Notion Calendar, Fantastical, Amie, Reclaim, and a few others. Each one promised to transform my relationship with scheduling.

Each one had the same failure mode: it became a system to maintain. Updates to sync. Configurations to manage. Occasional conflicts between the app's interpretation of my calendar and what was actually in Google Calendar. A new place to check when something looked wrong.

The productivity I was trying to improve was being consumed by the productivity tooling.

## The case for lightweight tools

A lightweight tool does one thing well and stays out of the way. For calendar access, that means: fast popup, accurate event data, join links accessible. Done.

No AI scheduling. No automatic time-blocking. No sync management. No premium features requiring a subscription to unlock the things you actually need.

The lightweight tool doesn't try to improve your calendar. It improves your access to it. The distinction is the entire point.

## What I gave up and didn't miss

**Natural language event creation.** I create events in Google Calendar directly. Typing "Meeting with Alex Thursday 2pm" into a special input field saves me nothing over using the keyboard shortcut in the actual calendar.

**AI scheduling suggestions.** I make my own scheduling decisions. I don't need a model to suggest when I should have lunch.

**Time analytics dashboards.** Interesting in theory, rarely actionable in practice. I know I have too many meetings. I don't need a chart to confirm that.

**Beautiful custom views.** The Google Calendar weekly view is good enough. A prettier version of the same information isn't a productivity improvement.

## What I kept

**A toolbar extension for quick access.** One click to see today's events and join links. That's the whole value proposition. Nothing more is needed.

> The right tool is the one that solves the actual problem without introducing new ones. For calendar access, lightweight almost always wins.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Heavy calendar apps solve feature gaps but add maintenance overhead; most people don't have feature gaps
- Lightweight extensions solve access friction without adding system complexity
- The right test: does this tool reduce the number of steps for something I do every day?
