# The Calendar Boundaries That Protect My Work-From-Home Sanity

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · May 21, 2027*

---

Work from home dissolves the natural boundaries that the office provides. The commute that created a physical transition between home-mode and work-mode is gone. The visible social signal of "I'm not in the office" is gone. The building that closed at 7pm is gone.

Without those external structures, the workday tends to expand indefinitely — and contracts into the evening, the weekend, and the mental space that used to be yours.

## What calendar boundaries actually are

A calendar boundary is a visible, committed block of time that marks the edge of work. Not just an intention to stop at 6pm — a calendar event called "Shutdown" at 5:30 that you treat with the same seriousness as a meeting.

Boundaries that live only in your head are invisible to the calendar, invisible to colleagues, and therefore invisible to the part of your brain that's always looking for one more thing to do before the day ends.

## The three boundaries worth putting on the calendar

**Work start signal.** A 10-minute "morning review" block that begins the workday. You're not available before this. The block starts the day — psychologically and practically.

**End of the deep work window.** A visible shift in the afternoon when deep work ends and the calendar opens to meetings and administrative work. For me, this is 4:30pm. After this point, I'm in "wrap up" mode, not "start something new" mode.

**End of the workday.** A "shutdown" block that is treated as a commitment. After this block, the laptop closes. Work communication pauses. The workday is done.

## What this looks like to colleagues

A shared calendar with these blocks tells colleagues something without requiring a conversation: there's a pattern to when I'm working, and boundaries I've communicated. The start and end blocks show up as busy — not impenetrable, but visible.

> The hardest part of work-from-home boundaries isn't setting them. It's keeping them when something "urgent" comes up at 7pm and the laptop is three feet away. The calendar blocks are a social contract with yourself, not just a schedule.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Work boundaries need calendar representation to be real — invisible boundaries are easy to ignore
- Three key blocks: work start signal, end-of-deep-work transition, end-of-day shutdown
- Sharing these blocks with colleagues communicates expectations without requiring a conversation
