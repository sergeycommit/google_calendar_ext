# One Calendar for Everything. Or Two? The Answer Isn't Obvious.

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · January 1, 2027*

---

Happy new year. This week's question is one I revisit every January: should my work and personal life live on the same calendar or separate ones?

I've tried both extensively. The answer turns out to depend less on ideology and more on what problems you're actually trying to solve.

## The case for one calendar

When everything is in one place, you get the full picture of your time at a glance. A doctor's appointment shows up in the same view as your 2pm meeting, so you can see immediately that the commute creates a conflict. A birthday dinner is visible when you're considering whether to accept a work event that evening.

The integration prevents invisible conflicts — the ones that only become apparent when both things are open on the same day.

## The case for separate calendars

Work calendars are often shared with colleagues. Sharing "dentist appointment" or "couples therapy" with your coworkers is probably not what you want.

The privacy concern is real and practical. Even with careful sharing settings, keeping personal events on a separate calendar that you control independently removes the possibility of accidental oversharing.

## The hybrid that works best

Google Calendar's multi-calendar system is built for this: you can have multiple calendars, each with different colors and sharing settings, all visible in the same view.

Work calendar: shared with colleagues, visible to your team, company events auto-added.
Personal calendar: private or shared only with family, personal appointments and events.
Both visible simultaneously in your main view.

This gives you the full picture without the privacy trade-off. The two calendars exist separately for permissions and sharing; they appear together for planning.

> The setup takes ten minutes: create a personal calendar alongside your work calendar, move personal events into it, set the sharing to private. Done.

## One more calendar worth considering

A "Goals and blocks" calendar for your time-blocking and project work. This separates your protective blocks from your meetings visually and makes it easier to share "free/busy" without sharing your personal work schedule details.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- A multi-calendar setup gives you unified view with separate privacy controls — the best of both approaches
- Personal calendar private, work calendar shared with appropriate permissions, blocks calendar optional
- The Google Calendar sidebar makes visibility per-calendar a simple toggle
