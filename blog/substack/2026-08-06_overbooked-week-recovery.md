# When the Week Is Already Overbooked: A Recovery Playbook

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · November 5, 2027*

---

Sometimes the week is already overbooked before it starts. Every slot filled, multiple overlaps, deadlines stacking on the same days. The preventive advice — protect your schedule, block time early, batch meetings — is all true, and none of it helps right now.

This is the recovery playbook: what to do when you're already in it.

## Step 1: Take stock of what's actually committed

Open the calendar and categorize every event into three buckets:

**Immovable:** External meetings with clients, customers, or people where cancelling would have real costs. Deadlines with contractual or business-critical consequences.

**Movable:** Internal meetings, syncs, and reviews that can be rescheduled within the week or pushed to next week without significant consequence.

**Cuttable:** Meetings or events you agreed to but which, looking at them honestly, can be declined or delegated.

The goal of this step is clarity. You're not making decisions yet — you're mapping the terrain.

## Step 2: Make the hard cuts

Move or cut every Movable and Cuttable item you can. Not every meeting is a real commitment. Some invites you accepted because refusing felt awkward. An overbooked week gives you the practical justification to decline what you should have declined earlier.

A brief note is enough: "I'm overbooked this week — is there any chance we can push this to [next week]?" Most people say yes. Most situations survive the delay.

## Step 3: Protect one block

In what remains, find one 90-minute window and protect it. Don't let it become another meeting. This block is for the most important thing that needs to happen this week. One thing, protected.

## Step 4: Do the post-mortem

After the week ends, ask: how did this happen? What accepted commitments created the stack? What could be declined more systematically going forward? The goal isn't punishment — it's structural improvement.

> An overbooked week is recoverable. An overbooked month is a systems problem.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- Categorize as immovable / movable / cuttable — the map precedes the cuts
- Decline movable items with a brief, honest note — most people accommodate
- Protect one block for the most important work even in a wreckage week
