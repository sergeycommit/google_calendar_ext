# Your Calendar Might Be Making You Burned Out

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · September 17, 2027*

---

Burnout has many causes, but one of the most consistent contributing factors is a specific kind of calendar problem: the calendar that never has space. Every hour committed. No recovery time. No transitions. No room for the unexpected.

Most people experiencing calendar-driven burnout don't identify the calendar as the cause — they identify it as the symptom. "I'm just so busy." But busyness is a calendar state before it's a feeling.

## The signs a calendar is contributing to burnout

**Consistent back-to-back scheduling.** No gaps between meetings. Context switches happen with zero recovery time. The day ends with a cognitive debt that accumulates week over week.

**Meetings on Mondays before 9:30am and Fridays after 3:30pm.** The calendar is consuming the margins of the week — the time that used to serve as mental transition.

**No visible deep work in the past two weeks.** You've been in reactive mode for so long you can't remember the last time you had a protected block to think.

**Recurring events that you dread.** Not just discomfort — genuine dread. Events you attend on autopilot without contributing or receiving value.

**No personal time visible.** Lunch not on the calendar. Exercise not on the calendar. The calendar is entirely work.

## What to do about it

The calendar problem requires a calendar solution. Several of the approaches in this newsletter apply directly:

**Audit recurring meetings.** Cancel or reduce the ones that produce dread without value.

**Build in buffer.** Even 10 minutes between meetings changes the texture of the day.

**Block at least 30 minutes of personal time daily.** Lunch, a walk, anything that isn't work. Make it visible on the calendar so it's as defensible as a meeting.

**Take one full day off without scheduling.** See what it reveals about what you were carrying.

> A calendar is not causing your burnout in isolation — but a damaged calendar is a compounding factor in almost every case I've seen. Fixing it doesn't solve everything, but it creates the space where recovery becomes possible.

- The calendar isn't the only cause of burnout, but it's a lever you control directly
