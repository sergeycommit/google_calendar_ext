# The Best Calendar Apps for Mac in 2028: An Honest Ranking

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · January 28, 2028*

---

I've used every major calendar app on Mac seriously enough to have opinions. Here's the honest version, without affiliate links or sponsored placements.

## The options worth considering

**Google Calendar (web, Chrome or Safari)**
This is where most knowledge workers should start. Full feature set, best-in-class sharing and team coordination, seamless integration with Google Workspace. The "app" is a browser tab. For most users, it's sufficient.
*Best for: Google Workspace users, anyone who needs team calendar sharing and management.*

**Apple Calendar (native macOS)**
The best native macOS integration: Spotlight search, Siri, Focus modes, system notifications. Works with Google Calendar (via account sync), Exchange, and iCloud simultaneously. Can't create events through Google Calendar's web interface, so significant calendar management should still happen there.
*Best for: Apple-ecosystem users who want native integration for quick reference.*

**Notion Calendar (formerly Cron)**
The best-designed third-party calendar app for Mac. Clean interface, excellent keyboard shortcuts, meeting prep surface that pulls in relevant documents. Syncs to Google Calendar. Beautiful and fast.
*Best for: Keyboard-driven workers who spend a lot of time in their calendar and value design quality.*

**Fantastical**
Mature, feature-rich, the original premium Mac calendar app. Natural language event creation is excellent. Paid subscription ($57/year). Overkill for most users but genuinely powerful for complex scheduling needs.
*Best for: Power users with complex multi-calendar setups who want maximum features.*

**Amie**
Integrates task management with calendar scheduling in a single view. Shows todos alongside events. For people who want their task list and calendar to be one tool.
*Best for: Task-and-calendar workflow users who want unified management.*

## My recommendation

**90% of users:** Use Google Calendar on the web + a browser extension for quick access. No installation needed, full features, best team coordination.

**Mac power users:** Connect Google Calendar to Apple Calendar for native notifications; use Notion Calendar if you want a better dedicated interface.

> The app isn't the bottleneck. Habits are.
