# My 5-Minute End-of-Day Calendar Review That Keeps Tomorrow Sane

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · January 22, 2027*

---

At some point I noticed that my most stressful mornings were preceded by evenings where I just closed the laptop without thinking about tomorrow. I'd wake up, see the calendar, discover a 9am meeting I hadn't prepared for, and spend the first hour of the day in catch-up mode.

The 5-minute end-of-day calendar review costs 5 minutes and returns several hours of calmer days.

## What happens in the 5 minutes

**Look at tomorrow.** Not the week — just tomorrow. What's on the calendar? What's the first thing, and is there anything I need to do before it?

**Flag preparation needs.** Is there a meeting tomorrow where I need to read something, find a document, or think through a position? If yes, I either do that now (if it takes 10 minutes) or schedule a prep block first thing in the morning.

**Check for surprises.** Did anything new appear on tomorrow's calendar that wasn't there this morning? Sometimes invites arrive during the day that create conflicts or require rescheduling.

**Note what didn't happen today.** Which tasks from today's plan didn't run? Do they need to move to tomorrow, or can they wait?

**Decide on a shutdown signal.** After the review, I close the laptop with the actual intention of being done for the day. The review serves as a natural endpoint — work is closed, tomorrow is prepared, there's nothing urgent left unaddressed.

## The psychological function

A lot of evening work-related rumination comes from unresolved uncertainty: "Is there something I'm forgetting? Is tomorrow going to be a disaster?" A brief review that answers those questions — even partially — reduces that background noise.

You're not solving tomorrow's problems at 6pm. You're confirming that you know what they are and that they'll be handled at the right time.

> The review is a different action than "checking email before bed." Email at 10pm generates new anxiety. A calendar review at 5:30pm closes existing anxiety.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- End-of-day calendar review looks at tomorrow and catches preparation needs while there's still time
- Note what didn't happen today and decide whether it moves or waits
- The review doubles as a natural work shutdown — a signal that the day is complete
