# Google Calendar vs. Outlook: The Honest Comparison Nobody Writes

*Schedule Calendar Weekly · December 3, 2027*

---

Most comparisons of Google Calendar and Outlook are written by people who haven't used both seriously, or who have a stake in making one look better than the other. This is an honest take from someone who has used both for extended periods.

The short answer: they're more similar than the tool wars suggest, and the right choice is almost entirely determined by your organizational context.

## Where they're genuinely different

**Integration ecosystems.** Google Calendar integrates seamlessly with Google Workspace — Gmail, Meet, Docs, Drive. Outlook integrates seamlessly with Microsoft 365 — Outlook email, Teams, SharePoint. If your organization uses one ecosystem, the calendar from that ecosystem is almost always the right choice.

**Mobile experience.** Google Calendar's mobile app is generally considered cleaner and more intuitive. Outlook's mobile app has more features but more complexity.

**Sharing and permissions.** Both support free/busy and full-detail sharing, but the mechanics differ. Google's sharing is simpler to set up; Outlook's is more configurable for complex organizational hierarchies.

**Event details and integrations.** Outlook events can embed rich content and integrate with more enterprise tools. Google Calendar events are simpler but connect better with consumer tools.

**Performance.** Google Calendar is faster on web. Outlook's web version lags on feature-heavy instances.

## Where they're similar

Recurring events, multiple calendar layers, timezone support, notification customization, basic scheduling — these features are comparable in both tools. The quality of day-to-day scheduling is not materially different.

## The actual decision

If your company uses Google Workspace: use Google Calendar. If your company uses Microsoft 365: use Outlook. If you're choosing personally with no organizational constraint: Google Calendar has a better consumer experience.

> The choice of calendar is rarely the bottleneck. How you use it is.

---

**This week's takeaway:**
- The calendar that integrates with your organizational ecosystem is almost always the right choice
- Google Calendar is simpler and faster on web; Outlook is more configurable for enterprise environments
- The quality of your scheduling habits matters more than which calendar tool you're using
