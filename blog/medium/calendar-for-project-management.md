# Using Your Calendar as a Light Project Management Tool

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/calendar-for-project-management.html)*

---

Project management has many tools purpose-built for it. The calendar is not one of them — but it holds the time dimension of a project, and that dimension matters more than most people give it credit for.

A calendar-based approach to light project management starts with a simple observation: a project has work that needs to happen at specific times, milestones that need to be visible on specific dates, and a rhythm of checkpoints and reviews. All of these are fundamentally calendar things.

Milestones work well as all-day events. In Google Calendar, all-day events appear at the top of each day's column in week view and stretch across the entire month cell in month view. They are visible without occupying a time slot, which means adding a 'Client proposal: final draft due' milestone to the calendar does not interfere with scheduling meetings or work blocks on that day — it simply marks the date as significant.

Work sessions go one step further. A milestone without time blocked before it is a deadline without a plan. Adding '2h: Write proposal draft' as a timed event a few days before the milestone closes the gap between 'I know when this is due' and 'I have time reserved to do it.'

The calendar can also carry project cadence — recurring events for weekly stand-ups, bi-weekly reviews, or monthly stakeholder updates. These keep the project rhythm visible without requiring a separate tool to manage them.

Color-coding helps. Using a consistent color for project events across your calendar makes them visually distinct from meetings and personal commitments. In week view, a pattern of project work blocks in one color and meetings in another gives you an immediate visual measure of how much time is going where.
