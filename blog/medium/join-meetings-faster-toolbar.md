# How to Join Meetings Faster With a Toolbar Calendar

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/join-meetings-faster-toolbar.html)*

---

The mechanics of joining a video meeting are more friction-filled than they need to be. You are in the middle of something. The calendar notification fires or a Slack message says 'joining now.' You switch to the calendar tab, find today, click the event, scroll to the join link, and click. By the time you are in the meeting, thirty seconds have passed and the first few sentences of conversation are behind you.

This is not a discipline problem. It is a workflow problem — the steps required to join a meeting are unnecessarily many, and their friction accumulates into a consistent pattern of arriving slightly after meetings start.

A toolbar calendar that shows conference links alongside events collapses this workflow. The meeting appears in the popup before it starts, with a clickable join link. When the countdown shows 'meeting starts in 0 minutes,' one more click puts you in the call. Total sequence: two clicks, from any browser tab.

The time savings per meeting join are modest — twenty to thirty seconds. The consistency improvement is not. Being reliably on time to meetings is a professional behavior that accumulates into a reputation over time. The colleagues who are consistently in the call when it starts, without scramble or apology, are noticed. The mechanism that produces that consistency is often simply better infrastructure, not better intentions.

The meeting-join improvement also enables better preparation. Seeing the upcoming meeting in the toolbar popup fifteen minutes before it starts — with the join link visible — is a natural preparation cue. You know the meeting is approaching, you know where the link is, and you have time to do anything the meeting requires before joining.
