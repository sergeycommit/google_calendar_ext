# Calendar Etiquette for Remote Teams

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/remote-team-calendar-etiquette.html)*

---

Remote teams depend on their calendars as the primary coordination mechanism in a way that co-located teams never fully do. In an office, there are dozens of informal coordination channels — walking by a desk, reading body language in the hallway, catching someone at the coffee machine. None of these exist in a distributed team. The calendar does the work that all of those informal channels do.

This means that the quality of calendar habits on a remote team directly determines the quality of coordination. A team where some members share their calendars and others do not, where invites sometimes include conference links and sometimes do not, and where working hours are opaque creates constant small frictions that accumulate into significant coordination overhead.

The most important norm is accuracy. A calendar that does not reflect its owner's actual commitments is a broken coordination tool. Meetings get scheduled into windows that are already occupied. Colleagues plan for a collaboration session that turns out to conflict with a commitment that was never added to the calendar. Keeping your calendar current is not just a personal productivity habit — it is a courtesy to everyone who depends on your schedule being readable.

The second most impactful norm is conference link consistency. Remote teams have no physical fallback. If a meeting is scheduled without a conference link, there is no option to just meet in conference room B. Every meeting invite should include a conference link before it is sent, not as an afterthought.

Time zone awareness is the third area where intentional norms pay dividends. The implicit assumption that scheduling meetings at the organizer's preferred time is fine works in a co-located context and breaks in a distributed one. Rotating who takes the inconvenient slot, documenting meeting times in multiple zones, and using Google Calendar's time zone tools all reduce the quiet resentment that builds when the same person always takes the 7 AM call.
