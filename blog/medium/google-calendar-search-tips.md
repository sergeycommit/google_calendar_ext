# How to Find Anything in Google Calendar Quickly

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/google-calendar-search-tips.html)*

---

Google Calendar search is one of those features that most people use at about ten percent of its capacity. Most people know you can type a name and find a meeting. Fewer know you can filter by attendee, date range, and calendar simultaneously to surface exactly the event you are looking for from a year ago.

The search field opens with the search icon in the top right of Google Calendar, or with the / shortcut if keyboard shortcuts are enabled. The search covers all calendars you have access to — personal, work, shared — and it includes past events by default. This makes it a useful retrospective tool, not just a forward-looking one.

The most underused capability is attendee filtering. After running a search, the results page shows filter options including a People field. Enter a name or email and the results narrow to only events where that person is an attendee. Before a first 1:1 with someone, or before reconnecting with a client you have not spoken to in months, this quickly surfaces your entire meeting history with them.

Date range filtering focuses the results on a specific period. This is faster than scrolling backward through the calendar to find an event from Q2 last year. Combine the text search with a date filter and you can locate almost any past event within a few seconds.

For this to work as a knowledge archive, you need the habit of adding context to event descriptions. Even two or three bullet points — what was decided, what was left open, who is responsible for what — turns your calendar into a searchable history of work. Searching 'API launch decision' surfaces the meeting where that decision was made, with the full context preserved.
