# Google Calendar vs. Apple Calendar: Which Fits Your Workflow?

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/google-calendar-vs-apple-calendar.html)*

---

Google Calendar and Apple Calendar share basic scheduling functionality but are designed for different ecosystems and different use cases.

Apple Calendar is the better choice for exclusive Apple device users. The native integration with iPhone, Mac, and iPad is genuinely excellent — Siri scheduling, notification handling, and iCloud sync work in ways that no cross-platform calendar can match. If everyone you share a calendar with also uses Apple devices, the experience is seamless.

Google Calendar is the better choice for cross-platform use and external collaboration. It works equally well on Windows, Android, and Apple devices. Its sharing settings are more flexible — you can share a calendar with anyone, control visibility levels, and collaborate with non-Google users through iCal links.

Many people use both: Google Calendar for work scheduling and external collaboration (where cross-platform and sharing matter), and Apple Calendar as the native aggregator on iPhone and Mac that displays all calendars in one place.

The hybrid approach is common and practical. Apple Calendar can subscribe to Google Calendar feeds and display them natively on Apple devices. The work calendar is managed in Google; the iPhone displays it through Apple Calendar.

For Chrome users who primarily work with Google Calendar, a compact browser extension reduces the tab-switching cost of staying aware of the schedule. This is a Google Calendar-specific enhancement — Apple Calendar does not have equivalent Chrome extension support.
