# Using Your Calendar to Set Work-From-Home Boundaries

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/work-from-home-calendar-boundaries.html)*

---

The office building handles work-life boundary enforcement in ways most people do not appreciate until they work from home. Arriving and leaving the building is a physical signal of transition. The commute is a decompression period. The absence of a laptop at home means work genuinely ends when you leave.

Working from home removes all of these. The laptop is always accessible. The work email is open. The natural endpoints are gone, and without deliberate replacement structure, work expands to fill whatever time is available — which in a connected home environment is a lot.

Calendar structure provides some of the scaffolding that the office environment previously supplied. Working hours set in Google Calendar make your availability window visible to colleagues. They see a warning when scheduling outside those hours. The warning does not prevent invites from being sent, but it shifts the social default — scheduling at 7 PM becomes a visible exception rather than a routine choice.

For a harder end-of-day boundary, a recurring 'Unavailable' event at your intended close time, marked as busy, creates a block that most meeting scheduling tools treat as occupied. This does not prevent a true emergency from reaching you — it prevents the casual late-afternoon meeting from becoming habitual without either party consciously choosing it.

Blocking lunch consistently does two things. It signals a genuine daily break, which is important for both sustained performance and well-being. And it prevents noon from becoming the default meeting slot. Lunch blocks erode easily if you let them — the first few exceptions feel reasonable, and gradually the break disappears. A recurring calendar block forces the decision to be explicit.

The personal-to-work-calendar question comes up often in this context. Personal commitments that affect your availability — a school pickup, a medical appointment, an afternoon you have committed to something outside work — should be on your work calendar as blocked time. If they are not visible, colleagues schedule into those windows without knowing they are already committed.
