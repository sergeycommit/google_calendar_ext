# How to Batch Shallow Work on Your Calendar

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/shallow-work-batching.html)*

---

Shallow tasks are not the enemy of focused work. Handling them continuously throughout the day is.

Email and Slack notifications, when they arrive in real time and are answered in real time, distribute their cognitive cost throughout the entire day. Every check is a small context switch. Every response is a brief pull away from whatever required sustained concentration.

Batching collects these tasks into defined windows — two or three per day — and protects the time between them for deeper work. Instead of answering emails as they arrive, you handle all email in 30–45 minute windows at 10am and 4pm. The messages are still answered; the interruptions are concentrated rather than continuous.

The mechanics in Google Calendar: create two recurring events per workday. Label them 'Email + Slack batch' or similar. Set them at consistent times. Between them, turn notifications off — not silent, off. The difference matters: silenced notifications still create a pull to check. No notifications remove the trigger.

What belongs in a batch: email, Slack, scheduling, quick reviews, expense reports, form submissions, anything that can be interrupted and resumed without significant cost.

What does not belong: writing, coding, analysis, complex decisions — these are deep work and need blocks, not batches.

The time between batches is your protected focus window. It does not require any additional scheduling; it emerges from having boundaries on the shallow work.
