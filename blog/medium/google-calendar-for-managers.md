# Google Calendar for Managers: Staying Available Without Losing Focus

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/google-calendar-for-managers.html)*

---

A manager's calendar is one of the highest-leverage tools available to them — and one of the most commonly under-managed. When a manager's calendar is reactive and fragmented, the team experiences a leadership bottleneck: decisions get delayed, questions go unanswered, and the strategic work that should be informing the team's direction gets squeezed out by the meeting load.

The core tension is real: managers need to be accessible to their teams and also need uninterrupted time for the thinking that makes that accessibility valuable. An entirely open calendar produces a manager who is always in meetings but never doing the strategic work those meetings are supposed to enable. An entirely blocked calendar produces a manager who is thinking deeply but unavailable to unblock their team.

The structural solution is intentional design that creates reliable access without fragmenting the entire day. Recurring 1:1s are the cornerstone — a standing weekly or bi-weekly slot with each direct report provides a predictable channel for questions, concerns, and decisions that reduces the pressure for ad-hoc scheduling requests. When direct reports know the next 1:1 is two days away, most non-urgent questions wait rather than arriving as interrupt requests.

Open hours — designated windows when any team member can book time without asking first — concentrate the ad-hoc availability into predictable periods. A manager with two-hour open hours on Tuesday and Thursday mornings is more reliably accessible to their team than a manager who is theoretically available any time but actually in meetings most of the day.

Strategic focus blocks — not for email, not for management tasks, but for the planning, thinking, and preparation that the role uniquely requires — are the third element. Two to three focused sessions per week, treated with the same respect as external meetings, are what produce the strategic clarity that makes the rest of the management calendar more effective.
