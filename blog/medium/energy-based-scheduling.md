# Energy-Based Scheduling: Align Your Calendar to Peak Hours

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/energy-based-scheduling.html)*

---

All hours are not equal. A 90-minute focus session at 9am is not the same as 90 minutes at 3pm after four consecutive meetings. Energy-based scheduling builds on this simple observation: match the hardest work to your best hours, and match the lowest-effort tasks to your depleted periods.

Finding your peak takes about a week of observation. When do you write or code most fluidly? When do you re-read the same sentence three times? The answers usually reveal a consistent 2–3 hour window — for many people in the morning, but genuinely variable between individuals.

Once you identify the window, protect it for deep work. Create a recurring block, set it to Busy, and treat it as structurally non-optional. During that window, do the work that most requires sustained concentration: writing, complex analysis, coding, strategic thinking.

Schedule meetings and collaborative work during your middle-energy period. Social and verbal tasks are often energizing rather than depleting at moderate energy levels, and they do not require the same cognitive load as deep thinking.

Batch administrative tasks — email, Slack, scheduling, expense reports — into your lowest-energy window. These can be done with reduced bandwidth and do not benefit from peak concentration.

The practical challenge is negotiating this against your organization's meeting culture. The most useful tool: block your peak hours as Busy before anyone can schedule there, and state a clear availability window for calls.
