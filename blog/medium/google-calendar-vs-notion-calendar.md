# Google Calendar vs. Notion Calendar: When Each Makes Sense

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/google-calendar-vs-notion-calendar.html)*

---

Google Calendar and Notion Calendar solve adjacent problems, not the same one.

Google Calendar is a scheduling and coordination tool. It is where meeting invites arrive, where shared team calendars live, and where external collaborators expect to find your availability. Its integration depth with Gmail, Google Meet, and the broader scheduling ecosystem is unmatched for coordination purposes.

Notion Calendar connects calendar events to Notion's database of pages and documents. A meeting with a client links to the client record. A project milestone appears in the calendar with context from the project page. For heavy Notion users, this context layer reduces the friction of moving between the schedule and the project context.

The practical approach for most people: use Google Calendar as the primary scheduling tool for external coordination, and use Notion Calendar as a supplementary planning view that adds context when you are working in Notion. Notion Calendar can display Google Calendar events, so the two views show the same schedule with different levels of context.

Notion Calendar is most valuable for people who already build most of their work context in Notion. It is less valuable for people who primarily coordinate externally or whose teams use Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 as the scheduling layer.

For Chrome-based work, a compact extension like Schedule Calendar can reduce the tab-switching overhead of monitoring Google Calendar while working in Notion or any other browser-based tool.
