# Google Calendar Sharing and Permissions Explained

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/google-calendar-sharing.html)*

---

Sharing a Google Calendar feels straightforward until the question of 'how much should I share?' comes up. The answer depends on who you are sharing with, what they need to see, and what you want to keep private — and Google Calendar gives you enough control to manage all of these, if you know where to look.

The fundamental mechanism is permission levels. When you share with a specific person, you choose one of four access levels: free/busy only, see all event details, make changes to events, or full administrative control. Each level serves a different relationship and a different need.

Free/busy is the appropriate default for broad sharing within an organization. It tells colleagues whether you are available or occupied without revealing what you are doing. Anyone scheduling a meeting with you gets what they need — your availability — without access to meeting titles, attendees, or descriptions.

See all event details is the right level for close teammates, direct reports, or anyone who needs to understand your schedule fully to coordinate with you. It shows everything: event titles, descriptions, attendee lists, and conference links. Be aware that this level exposes personal events if they are on the same calendar.

The practical solution to the personal-professional overlap is simple: maintain separate calendars. A work calendar shared at 'see all event details,' a personal calendar shared at 'free/busy only' or not shared at all. Events on the personal calendar are private by default while your work calendar remains transparent to your team.

For team coordination, a shared team calendar — a separate calendar that multiple people can add events to — is often cleaner than sharing individual calendars. It keeps group events in one place without exposing individual schedules beyond what each person chooses to share.
