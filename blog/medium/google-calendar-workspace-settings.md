# Google Calendar for Google Workspace Teams: Key Settings

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/google-calendar-workspace-settings.html)*

---

Most Google Calendar users in a Workspace environment are using it with its default settings — which are sensible defaults but not necessarily the best configuration for your actual work style or team's scheduling needs.

At the individual level, the most impactful settings are working hours, default event duration, and world clock. Setting working hours signals to colleagues when you are available without requiring them to guess or ask. The system shows a warning when they try to schedule outside those hours — a gentle friction that prevents early-morning or late-evening invites without any confrontation.

Changing the default event duration from 30 minutes to 25 is one of those small changes with disproportionate effects. Every meeting you create automatically has a five-minute buffer built in. Over a week of meetings, this adds up to real transition time that would otherwise not exist.

The world clock feature is underused in distributed teams. Adding a secondary time zone to the calendar sidebar means you always see both your local time and the reference time zone of your remote teammates. This eliminates the constant mental arithmetic of 'what time is that for them?' when scheduling across zones.

At the Workspace admin level, the most important settings for most organizations are the sharing defaults — controlling whether calendars are visible within the organization by default, and what level of detail is shown — and the external sharing policy, which determines whether users can share calendars with people outside the company.

For teams that manage physical meeting spaces, room resources are worth configuring properly. Rooms as calendar resources give everyone a single place to check availability and book spaces, which eliminates the friction of separate room-booking systems.
