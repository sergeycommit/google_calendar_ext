# Setting Up Out-of-Office and Focus Time in Google Calendar

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/google-calendar-out-of-office.html)*

---

Google Calendar's Out of Office and Focus Time event types are two of the most genuinely useful features that most Workspace users have but rarely set up correctly.

Out of Office goes beyond marking a time block as busy. When you create an Out of Office event and someone sends you a meeting invitation for a time within that period, Google Calendar automatically declines the invitation with a message that includes your custom text and return date. This transforms absence management from a manual task — declining each invite individually — into an automatic system.

The custom message is worth writing thoughtfully. 'Back on [date] and will review messages when I return' gives organizers everything they need without any ambiguity. Including the return date prevents the follow-up messages asking when you will be back.

Focus Time serves a different purpose. It does not auto-decline meetings — instead, it signals to colleagues that a block is committed to focused work, and optionally activates Do Not Disturb in Google Chat during that window. This makes it the right tool for recurring deep work sessions that you want visible on your calendar as intentional commitments rather than empty time.

The practical approach is to use both regularly. Focus Time blocks for daily or weekly deep work windows. Out of Office for actual absences — vacation days, conference days, any time you genuinely will not be responding. Together, they create a calendar that actively manages your availability rather than passively displaying it.
