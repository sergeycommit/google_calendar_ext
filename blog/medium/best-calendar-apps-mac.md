# Best Calendar Apps for Mac Users in 2026

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/best-calendar-apps-mac.html)*

---

Mac users have three practical calendar approaches: the native Apple Calendar, Google Calendar in Safari or Chrome, and Fantastical as a premium native alternative.

Apple Calendar is the strongest choice for users who primarily use Apple devices and share calendars with other Apple users. The native integration with macOS Notifications, Spotlight, Siri, and seamless iCloud sync across iPhone, Mac, and iPad produces an experience that no cross-platform tool can fully replicate. The limitation: cross-platform access is limited and sharing with non-Apple users requires workarounds.

Google Calendar works fully in Safari and Chrome on Mac. For users in Google Workspace environments, or who regularly work across different devices and platforms, Google Calendar's cross-platform consistency is a significant advantage. External sharing is simpler, team calendars are well-supported, and the web app is feature-complete.

Fantastical is the premium native option for Mac users who want more than Apple Calendar provides — natural language event entry, a unified multi-source view, and a well-regarded design. It is subscription-based.

The most common practical combination: Apple Calendar as the native aggregator on iPhone and Mac (displaying all calendar sources including the Google ones in one native interface), and Google Calendar for work scheduling and external collaboration where cross-platform access and sharing matter.

For Chrome users on Mac, Google Calendar extensions work identically as on other platforms. Schedule Calendar adds a compact toolbar popup and time-to-next-event display — a consistent tool across all operating systems.
