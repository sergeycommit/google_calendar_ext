# Google Calendar Keyboard Shortcuts That Save Real Time

*Originally published on [Schedule Calendar Blog](https://calendar-extension.site/blog/google-calendar-keyboard-shortcuts.html)*

---

Keyboard shortcuts are one of those tools that seem optional until you use them consistently for a week. Then switching back to mouse navigation feels inefficient in a way that is hard to ignore.

Google Calendar's shortcut system is comprehensive but not complicated. The most useful shortcuts fall into two groups: navigation and creation. Navigation shortcuts let you move through the calendar without lifting your hands from the keyboard. Creation shortcuts let you add events quickly without clicking through a multi-step form.

Before any of this works, you need to enable shortcuts. Google Calendar does not activate them by default. Go to Settings (the gear icon), find General, scroll to Keyboard shortcuts, and toggle them on. From that point, pressing the question mark key at any time shows the full list — you never need to memorize all of them at once.

For navigation, start with three: T for today, W for week view, and J or N to move to the next week. These three keys handle the majority of calendar navigation. Add K or P to go back a period and you have covered nearly every browsing action without touching the mouse.

For creation, C opens the full event editor. Q opens a quick-add field where you type natural language — 'budget review Thursday 3pm 45 minutes' — and Google Calendar parses it into a properly formatted event. The Q shortcut is worth practicing specifically; it reduces a three-click process to a single keystroke and a sentence.

The practical approach to building the habit: pick three shortcuts and use them deliberately for one week. T, C, and W are the best starting set. Once those are automatic, add Q and /. The learning curve is genuinely short — most people reach comfortable fluency in five working days of conscious practice.
