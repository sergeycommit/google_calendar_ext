Google Calendar and Outlook Calendar are not interchangeable — they are built on different philosophies and optimized for different ecosystems.

Google Calendar is browser-native and excellent for Google Workspace users. Sharing is granular and easy, Google Meet integration is one click, and the interface is fast to use without installing software. For individuals and small teams not locked into a Microsoft environment, it is the default choice.

Outlook Calendar belongs to Microsoft 365. For organizations using Exchange, Teams, and SharePoint, Outlook's integration advantages are significant: room booking systems, meeting propagation, and Teams calls are all Exchange-native. Fighting against this integration in a Microsoft-first organization costs more than any individual feature benefit.

The practical rule: use the calendar that integrates with your email and collaboration stack. If that is Google Workspace, Google Calendar. If that is Microsoft 365, Outlook Calendar. Individual feature comparisons rarely change this calculus.

For users who live primarily in Chrome and use Google Calendar, a compact extension can reduce the tab-switching cost of staying aware of the schedule. Schedule Calendar provides a toolbar popup with time-to-next-event visibility — a complement to Google Calendar, not a replacement.
