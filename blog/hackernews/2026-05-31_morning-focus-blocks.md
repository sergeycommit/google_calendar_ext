# The Case for Protecting Morning Hours Before Anything Else Gets Scheduled

*Hacker News post · May 31, 2026*

---

There's a straightforward observation that most people acknowledge but don't act on: cognitive performance is not uniform across the day. For most people, the late morning (8:30–11:30 for early risers, somewhat later for others) is when working memory capacity, decision quality, and creative output are highest. This peak is finite and non-renewable within the day.

Meetings scheduled during the peak consume it. Work scheduled outside the peak produces lower-quality output. The optimal policy — protect the peak for work, put meetings outside it — is obvious in theory and rarely followed in practice.

## Why the obvious policy fails

**Meetings are default-scheduled, work blocks are not.** A meeting request fills a slot automatically. Work blocks require a deliberate decision to create. Since meetings fill available time, they fill the peak first unless the peak is explicitly defended.

**Inboxes and Slack open during the morning peak.** Even without meetings, opening communications during the focus window invites the same fragmentation.

**"Morning" meetings feel normal.** A 9am standup, a 10am check-in — these feel like reasonable meeting times. They consume exactly the window they're consuming.

## The implementation

**Schedule the deep work block first, before the week starts.** Sunday evening: put 8:30–10:30 (or whatever your peak is) into the calendar as a recurring block. Mark it Busy. Before the week's meeting requests arrive, the slot is claimed.

**Delay inbox opening.** Notifications off or ignored until the block ends. This is the version that feels extreme but has disproportionate impact.

**Let the block name be specific.** "Write auth service spec" rather than "morning focus." Specific names create mild commitment.

The data on this is consistent: workers who protect morning hours for focused work report higher output quality and lower end-of-day fatigue. It's one of the higher-leverage scheduling changes available.
