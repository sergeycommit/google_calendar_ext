# Buffer Time Between Meetings: Quantifying the Cost of Not Having It

*Hacker News post · January 24, 2027*

---

The Microsoft Human Factors Lab published a study in 2021 measuring brain activity (EEG) across back-to-back meetings vs. meetings with 10-minute breaks. The findings: back-to-back meetings produced continuously increasing beta wave activity (a marker of stress) across the day. Meeting series with breaks showed stress returning near-baseline between sessions.

This is physiological, not motivational. Buffer time isn't a nicety; it's a recovery window that determines the quality of the subsequent meeting.

## What 10 minutes enables

**Context release and reorientation:** The previous meeting's concerns — decisions, tensions, unresolved items — need a brief period to deprioritize before a new context can take precedence. Without this, mental threads from meeting A contaminate meeting B.

**Working memory consolidation:** Immediately after a meeting, what was decided and what you committed to is in working memory. 5 minutes of note-taking captures it accurately. Without this window, notes written later capture what you *think* was decided.

**Physiological reset:** Standing, walking, changing posture — these change cortisol levels and attention quality in measurable ways. The effect is real even if brief.

## The implementation options

**Speedy meetings (Google Calendar):** Settings → General → Speedy meetings. Automatically ends 60-min meetings at :50 and 30-min meetings at :25. Creates buffer without any per-meeting negotiation. This is the zero-cost structural fix.

**Manual transition blocks:** 10-15 minute calendar events marked Busy between meeting clusters. The Busy status prevents the gap from being used for another meeting.

**Negotiated starts:** Proposing "10:10" instead of "10:00" as the meeting start. Less friction than reducing duration, same buffer effect.

## The ROI calculation

At 5 meetings per day, 10 minutes of buffer costs 50 minutes. The benefit: meeting quality is maintained across the day rather than degrading. On a 5-meeting day, the 4th meeting's quality degradation due to accumulated stress is worth more than 10 minutes of recovered attention.
