# Meeting Scheduling: The Invite as the First Signal of Meeting Quality

*Hacker News post · December 27, 2026*

---

The quality of a meeting correlates with the quality of its invite. Not perfectly — but the correlation is strong enough that improving invites is a high-leverage intervention that costs almost nothing.

## The signals in a meeting invite

**Duration.** Default is 60 minutes. If you scheduled 60 minutes because that's the calendar increment, not because 60 minutes is the right amount of time, the meeting will run 60 minutes regardless of whether it's done at 40. Schedule the duration your agenda requires, not the nearest increment.

**Title.** "Sync," "check-in," and "catch-up" are not meeting titles — they're meeting vibes. "Q4 budget review — final approval needed" tells attendees what to prepare, what role they play, and what success looks like.

**Description.** The description is where agenda goes. Even one bullet point changes how people prepare. Three structured bullets ("Context / Goal / Preparation needed") is better than a paragraph.

**Advance notice.** Invites sent the same day signal that the meeting isn't important enough to plan around. Invites sent 48+ hours before give people real preparation time.

**Invite list construction.** The default behavior — invite everyone who might be relevant — produces bloated attendee lists. For every person on the list: "what specifically does this person contribute to or receive from this meeting?" If the answer isn't specific, they can receive the notes instead.

## The test for a well-formed invite

Can a new attendee, reading only the invite, answer these four questions?
1. What is the purpose of this meeting?
2. What should I prepare or review in advance?
3. What will success look like at the end of this meeting?
4. Why am I specifically invited?

If yes: good invite. If not: the meeting isn't ready to be scheduled.

## The cost of doing this

Adding an agenda to a calendar invite takes 3 minutes. The meeting it produces takes 15 minutes less and makes 3 better decisions.
