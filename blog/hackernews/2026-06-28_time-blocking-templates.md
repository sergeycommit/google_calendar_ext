# Time Blocking Templates: Three Starting Configurations for Different Work Styles

*Hacker News post · June 28, 2026*

---

One of the friction points in starting time blocking is the blank calendar problem: you understand the principles but don't know how to configure a week. Templates remove this friction.

Three templates that cover most knowledge worker profiles:

## Template 1: Engineering/IC (deep work-heavy)

**Monday/Wednesday/Friday:**
- 8:30–10:30: Deep work (primary project, specific task)
- 10:30–11:00: Shallow work batch (email, Slack)
- 11:00–12:30: Second deep work or code review
- 2:00–4:00: Meeting window
- 4:30–5:00: Shallow work batch

**Tuesday/Thursday (meeting-heavy):**
- 8:30–10:00: Deep work (protected even on meeting days)
- 10:00–5:00: Meeting window, shallow work in gaps

Recurring: Friday 4:00–4:30 as weekly planning review.

## Template 2: Manager/Lead (meeting-heavy with strategy needs)

**Monday/Wednesday:**
- 8:30–10:00: Strategy/thinking block (no meetings)
- 10:00–5:00: Meetings, 1-on-1s, collaborative work

**Tuesday/Thursday:**
- Full meeting days, 1-on-1s clustered
- 4:30–5:00: Preparation review for next day

**Friday:**
- 8:30–11:00: Weekly planning + inbox zero + writing
- Afternoons: lighter meeting load

## Template 3: Freelancer/solo (variable client work)

- 8:30–10:00: Client A work (highest-priority client)
- 10:00–10:30: Shallow batch (email, proposals, invoicing)
- 10:30–12:00: Client B work
- 1:00–3:00: Client C/new project/learning
- 3:00–3:30: Shallow batch
- 3:30–5:00: Open / client calls / business development

All templates use: Busy status on focus blocks, specific task names, 60-70% booking rule.

## Implementation note

These are starting templates. The actual value comes from running one for two weeks, identifying what doesn't fit, and adjusting. The template is a hypothesis; your calendar data is the experiment.
