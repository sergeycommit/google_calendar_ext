# Back-to-Back Meetings: The Hidden Cost and Structural Fixes

*Hacker News post · October 4, 2026*

---

There's published research on this (Microsoft, 2021; Zijlstra et al., multiple) that's worth citing: back-to-back video meetings produce measurable stress accumulation that does not reset between meetings. EEG studies show increasing frontal alpha/beta asymmetry (a marker of stress) across consecutive meetings, with no recovery between them.

In contrast, the same number of meetings with 10-minute breaks shows stress markers that return near baseline between meetings.

This is a structural problem with a structural fix.

## What 10-minute gaps provide

**Context switching time.** Releasing the previous meeting's mental context and orienting to the next one's requires a physical moment. Without it, you carry residual threads from one into the other.

**Note consolidation.** Working memory holds the meeting's outcomes for a brief window. A 5-minute buffer to write down decisions and next steps is more accurate than memory captured 4 hours later.

**Physical state reset.** Standing, walking, changing environment — these produce measurable physiological changes that affect attention quality in the subsequent meeting.

## The structural implementation

**Speedy meetings (Google Calendar setting).** Found under Settings → General → Event settings → Speedy meetings. Automatically shortens 60-minute meetings to 50 minutes and 30-minute meetings to 25 minutes. Creates buffer without per-meeting negotiation.

**Manual transition blocks.** 10-minute recurring events labeled "Transition / Notes" between clustered meetings. Mark as Busy to prevent fill-in.

**Midday hard stop.** On days with 4+ meetings, a 30-minute genuine break at midday. Not email, not Slack — actual disengagement.

## The cost argument

The argument against: "I don't have time to waste 10 minutes between every meeting." The counter: the 10 minutes aren't waste — they're overhead that prevents 30-40% productivity degradation in the second half of a meeting-heavy day. The math is straightforward.
