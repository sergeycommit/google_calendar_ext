# Calendar Extension vs. Full Calendar App: When Each Is the Right Tool

*Hacker News post · July 25, 2027*

---

The question of whether to use a browser extension or a full calendar app (Fantastical, Notion Calendar, Amie) for Google Calendar is a make-vs-buy style analysis: you're deciding whether to switch tools or extend your existing one.

## When to use a full calendar app

A full calendar app is the right choice when:

**You need features Google Calendar doesn't have.** Natural language processing with higher accuracy, sophisticated recurring event patterns, advanced keyboard navigation beyond what Google provides, complex multi-account management, time tracking integration. If the feature you need is genuinely missing from Google Calendar, a full app fills the gap.

**Interface quality is a daily bottleneck.** If you're spending meaningful time in the calendar planning view and Google Calendar's UI is a genuine friction point, a better-designed interface has real ROI. Notion Calendar's keyboard shortcuts and Fantastical's event creation UX are materially better than Google Calendar's.

**You have complex multi-service requirements.** If you're managing Google Calendar + iCloud + Exchange simultaneously, a unified interface is worth the overhead.

## When a browser extension is sufficient

A browser extension is the right choice when:

**The problem is access speed, not features.** If your daily friction is "checking the calendar requires a tab switch," the problem isn't missing features — it's that the tool you need is one click away instead of zero clicks. An extension solves this without migration cost.

**Google Calendar already does everything you need.** Most knowledge workers don't have feature gaps in Google Calendar. They have access friction. These are different problems.

**You want zero migration cost.** Installing an extension takes 30 seconds and doesn't change your workflows, data, or how other people interact with your calendar. Switching to a full app requires learning a new interface, potential sync configuration, and some workflow change.

## The practical recommendation

Try the extension first. It's lower friction and lower risk. If after two weeks you have a specific feature need that the extension doesn't address, then evaluate full apps against that specific need.
